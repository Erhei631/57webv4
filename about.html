✸ Our story

Fifty-seven startups, and a name.

The team
Horizon Software → Symbio → Adobe Advertising Cloud

Our core team has worked together for more than twenty years, across five companies and two acquisitions. We built Horizon Software, which was acquired by Symbio. At Symbio we ran engineering for startups. We then joined TubeMogul, which Adobe acquired, and ran engineering inside Adobe Advertising Cloud.

Fifty-seven
Fifty-seven pre-seed startups

The idea for the company came from a number we could not forget. At Symbio, we worked with fifty-seven pre-seed startups. We watched most of them fail and a few succeed spectacularly, and we learned what actually separates the two. More than once, a founder offered us equity. We took cash instead. We should have taken the equity.

2018
57Blocks begins

In 2018 we spun out of Adobe to start 57Blocks. That experience taught us something about the industry we work in. Startups and services firms suffer from an impedance mismatch. A founder wants the best engineers, the lowest cost, and the fastest path to launch. A services firm is built to maximize revenue and grow the team. Those incentives pull in opposite directions, and the client feels it.

Today
Alignment, not project management

The way to fix it is not better project management. It is alignment. Take equity instead of cash, put skin in the game, and suddenly we want exactly what the founder wants: the smallest, best team, shipping the right thing fast. If the startup wins, the equity is worth far more than any services margin. That belief still shapes how we engage today.

We started 57Blocks in 2018 to take those lessons into frontier technology, beginning with blockchain. Fifty-seven startups, building in blocks. That is the name.

✸ What guides us

Four values, held daily.

These are not poster words. They are the operating habits that make a services business worth trusting and a frontier-tech business worth keeping.

Reliable

言行一致, words and actions align. We do what we said we would do, in the way we said we would do it. Every durable client relationship we have was built this way, one kept commitment at a time. In a services business, reliability is not a virtue on top of the work. It is the work.

Responsive

Being reachable, to clients and to each other, is how people learn they can count on you. Questions get answered, blockers get raised early, and nobody on either side of the engagement is left waiting in the dark.

Flexible

Frontier technology means most things have not been figured out yet. The plan will change, the tooling will change, sometimes the problem itself will change. We treat that as the nature of the work rather than a disruption to it, and we experiment instead of hardening around a first answer. Rigidity is how frontier products fail.

High quality

In the AI era, when anyone can generate output, taste and judgment are the differentiators. We hold ourselves to a standard the tools alone cannot reach: knowing what good looks like, and refusing to ship what falls short.

The thesis

The services business is a bootloader. It funds and feeds an engine that compounds: an AI-native delivery system, a knowledge layer of engineering patterns that deepens over time, and an equity portfolio built by placing world-class engineering teams inside the companies we believe in.

Operator first, vendor second

We hold equity in what we build, run our own systems in production, and invest alongside our clients. That changes the incentives. The architecture we ship is the architecture we live with.
