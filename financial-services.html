In finance, trust and correctness are the product.

Money movement has no tolerance for ambiguity. Regulated workloads, throughput, correctness, and audit traceability are unforgiving, and the bar is the same whether the rails are traditional or on-chain. The patterns below recur across the financial platforms we build.

✸ The stack

The on-chain money movement stack, and where we sit in it.

Money moves on-chain in layers, and we are engineered to interoperate across them. Above the rails sits the app the user touches, and beneath it a wallet, an issuer, a credit layer, a payment rail, and a compliance envelope at every step.

The app layer: the product users touch

Private-market and alternative investment platforms, SME lending and working-capital apps, and the onboarding and portfolio experiences on top.

Wallets: the entry point

Custody, key management, embedded wallets, and account abstraction.

Stablecoin issuance: the asset itself

Minting, redemption, reserves, and integration with traditional banking.

Credit and RWA: the financing layer

Receivables financing, private credit, tokenized real-world assets, and collateral management.

Payments and cards: the exit

Card issuance, settlement, and reconciliation at merchant and card-network scale.

Compliance: the trust layer

KYC and KYB, sanctions screening, AML, and transaction monitoring as the envelope around every layer.
